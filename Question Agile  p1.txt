Q4.What is Sprint Velocity
Sprint Velocity is the amount of work a team completes in one sprint, usually measured in story points.
Example:
Sprint 1 → Completed 20 story points
Sprint 2 → Completed 25 story points
Sprint 3 → Completed 30 story points

👉 Average Sprint Velocity = (20 + 25 + 30) / 3 = 25 story points

So, your team can handle around 25 story points per sprint.

Q17 Diff scrum and kunbun
*Workflow: Scrum uses fixed-length Sprints. Kanban is a continuous
*flow.Roles: Scrum requires specific roles (Scrum Master, Product Owner, Team). Kanban has no required roles.
*Changes: Scrum generally prohibits changes during a sprint. Kanban allows changes at any time.
*Metrics: Scrum measures performance using velocity (story points per sprint). Kanban measures efficiency using cycle time (time to complete a task).
*Boards: Scrum boards are cleared after each sprint, while Kanban boards are used continuously.
